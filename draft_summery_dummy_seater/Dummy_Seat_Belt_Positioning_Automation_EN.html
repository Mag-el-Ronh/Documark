üßç Step 1: Dummy on Seat Positioning

Goal

  • Position the virtual dummy on the seat with anatomically correct posture and regulatory alignment.

Sub-steps

  • Extract Seat Reference Geometry: Identify H-point, cushion surface, and backrest orientation.
  • Align Dummy Pelvis & Torso: Position pelvis at H-point and orient torso and limbs based on seat angles and posture rules.
  • Apply Posture Rules: Use anthropometric data and regulation specs (e.g., Euro NCAP) to define joint angles and limb placement.
  • Validate Pose: Check head height, knee angle, and overall alignment. Log positioning metrics for reproducibility.

ü™ë Step 2: Seat Preloading

Goal

  • Simulate realistic seat deformation and occupant settling using a shelf-form Regal dummy.
  • Ensure accurate contact forces and boundary conditions for downstream crash analysis.

Sub-steps

  • Import & Validate Seat Geometry: Load seat model (cushion, backrest, frame) and verify mesh quality, material setup, and connectivity.
  • Apply Dummy Position from Step 1: Use Regal dummy in shelf-form configuration. Transfer joint locations and enforce posture via joint constraints or prescribed motion.
  • Activate Gravity & Mass: Introduce dummy weight and gravity to simulate natural settling. Ensure stable contact with seat surfaces.
  • Contact & Constraint Setup:
    • Define surface-to-surface contact with friction between dummy and seat.
    • Fix seat mounts and apply joint constraints to maintain anatomical posture.
  • Run Preload Simulation: Perform quasi-static analysis to capture seat compression and dummy settling. Monitor contact forces and posture evolution.
  • Export Preloaded State: Save deformed seat and dummy configuration for use in crash setup. Preserve contact and constraint definitions as needed.

Goal
